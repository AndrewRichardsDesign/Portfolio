Edit

Our task was to translate customization features from our Member Home Web redesign and those existing in the current app. We utilized AppX components, and native interactions for iOS, Android, and iPad.

Getting In and Out of Edit Mode

'edit' buttons on each product card (Banking, Insurance, etc) serve as entry points to the same Edit experience, where products from all categories can be edited. Tapping 'Done' at the top right takes the member back to the home page.

Base Page

The edit experience lives in a standard modal sheet, for iOS. It's one space that reflects the product list, arrangement, and organization of Home. From here, a member can edit and organizr any of their accounts and policies.

Edit an Account or Policy

Tapping an account or policy table cell navigates members down one level in the architecture where they can rename a product, hide a balance, or hide the product itself. Rather than include an explicit confirmation button, our lighter weight approach saves edits in real time so members only have to tap back, their natural last step of this view.

Reorder Products and Sections

An intuitive drag and drop interaction allows products to be reordered within a section and even to other sections. Entire cards can also be reordered.

Create a Group

Tapping the 'Create group' button opens a new modal sheet where members can either choose the default name or nickname their new group. Tapping 'Done' closes the modal sheet and displays the new group as a table cell at the end of its respective section card.

Add Items to a Group

Adding accounts and policies to a group utilizes the same drag and drop interaction seen above. Since this kind of organization is essentially arranging a list, the interaction can be just that.

Rename a Group

Utilizing that familiar model of navigating down a level within the Edit modal, members are able to rename their group. Also aligned to account editing, tapping 'back' is the only action necessary when the task is complete. An 'Ungroup' button also lives on this page, being one of two methods for 'ungrouping'.

Ungroup a Group

The alternate method for 'ungrouping' a group uses the typical iOS delete treatment. Tapping the red icon slides the cell over to reveal an 'Ungroup' button which removes the group title and returns the previously grouped items to the bottom of the list. If the member expresses hesitation they may also find the ungroup method in the edit group page, which provides clarifying text. Members are able to create any number of groups, rearrange them amongst themselves or move them to other sections via the drag and drop interaction.
