Organization and Interaction

The app utilizes a tab bar organization divided into three spaces of Home, Search, and Chat. Our structure also divides experiences into navigation views and modal sheets.

High Level Structure

At a high level, the app's structure consists of first level pages accessed by tab bar, child navigation views, and modal sheets.

Home, Search, and Chat

The app's first level experiences are Home, Search, and Chat, all accessible via the tab bar. If we zoom into Home, being a specific experience my team designed, it has it's own sub-structure. Home combines accounts, policies, quick actions, product acquisition, marketing, messaging, activity cards, and entry points to Profile and Edit. This is a very similar structure to what we designed for the website's homepage.

Navigating to Account Summary

Every account and policy has a summary page. They're accessed by tapping an account or policy and the summary experiences live within navigation views. My team collaborated with our design language team on the account summary design, creating a baseline that other teams can build from.

Servicing Actions, like Transfer

All of the buttons that appear below card titles are called servicing actions (Transfer, Pay Bills, Deposit, Send etc). How these experiences display is ultimately up to each individual experience team, but in the design of the app's overall view type strategy, we assigned modal sheets to many of these servicing actions.

Modal sheets are great for focused tasks or flows, like many of our servicing actions and contribute to the quick, lightweight feeling of an app. Another call out is the business requirement to include an access point to the Chat experience in every modal. It might seem repetitive, but another team flagged global access to Chat as a need. This is a great example of how teams shape each other's work.

Product Acquisition

Our menus provide a way for members to see what USAA has to offer, quickly deep dive to a product category, or start the process of connecting an account at an external institution. These menus, like most servicing actions, were also a great fit for modal sheets because they're focused, navigate to child pages within the modal, and hand off to webviews which we designed to look identical to iOS modal sheets.
