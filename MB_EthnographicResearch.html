Ethnographic Research

After compiling secondary research, we traveled to Brazil to conduct interviews, focus groups, and workshops to better understand what matters to Brazilian millennials, especially realted to their careers, impressions of banks, and relationships with products.

All of the information gathered on this trip was synthesized into ideas and strategies to our client attract millennials to the workplace and integrate millennial values into their product experiences.

Workshops

In Porto Alegre and Sao Paulo, we talked to people about their careers, experience with products, and perspectives on insurance. The workshop consisted of a few parts, including surveys, a research presentation, co-creation exercises, and interviews.

Sorting Priorities

Starting from our previous research, participants prioritized these career values and in some cases added their own. They were asked to explain their choices and provide personal examples of when they experienced or lacked a particular value.

Ideal workplace

Participants formed groups based on their top career values and collaboratively designed a workplace that focused on that chosen value.

Impression of a bank

In this exercise, we asked participants to write the words they immediately associate with the word ‚Äúbank‚Äù. After a conversation on these initial thoughts, we asked the participants if they can imagine themselves working in a bank and what they would change to make it more appealing.

User Experience

This activity explored why millennials use certain products and which traits are most valued in a banking product experience. Participant groups chose a product from the survey shown earlier, described it in detail, explained how the product integrates into their lives and discussed why those products are valuable to them.

Trend research

We identified a wide range of millennial trends by exploring how millennials work, communicate, use their free time, and interact with money. These trends were presented to our workshop participants to prepare participants for a product design exercise. Our intent was to validate the trends we identified applied to Brazilian millennials and discover new trends by exploring product experiences Brailians are engaged in.

Design a product

After our crash course in millennial trends, participant groups utilized the trends as a starting point to design a financial product or service.

Interviews

We led focus groups with people in Porto Alegre and Sao Paulo. Here, we asked more in depth questions about the workplace, their career, evolving values, and perception of banks.
