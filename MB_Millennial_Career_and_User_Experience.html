Millennial Career and User Experience

After conducting extensive secondary research into millennial social and career trends, we traveled to Brazil to conduct primary research into the local perception of banks and career values with our target audience.

We then created concepts, processes, and tools designed to improve workplace appeal and inform the product design process. These deliverables provide the opportunity for millennial values to be integrated throughout employee and customer experiences.

Keeping Millennials In the Workplace

We developed a strategy and supporting concepts that shift company culture to meet millennial needs. We investigated what millennials seek in a workplace, what makes them stay, and what convinces them to leave.

Our strategy draws from research and explores concepts within seven topics of Learning, Flexibility, Wellbeing, Money, Passion, Social, and Identity. Our concepts are wide ranging and touch on where and when people work, how coworkers engage with each other, and how they find meaning at work.

Attracting Millennials To The Workplace

Before our client could capture prospective employees' attention with all the benefits and innovative culture they offer, they had a larger problem to solve...a general negative image. This image was created by the what the public experiences; marketing, customer experience at branches, and using banking products. We found that it was hard for a lot of millennials to feel excitement working for a bank, especially one that was perceived as old, inefficient, and falling behind innovative competitors. Generally, millennials could not imagine working in any other capacity than at the branch.

Creating a Millennial Product

After identifying the product experience as an additional area of opportunity for our client, we developed a system to put our research findings to work. We created a process and tool for analyzing their products and then generating concepts. This can plug into the typical design process and help teams assess to what degree their offerings align to millennial values, then inspire new ideas that integrate those values.

We stress tested our system with case studies from six companies, documenting our assessment and concepts.
