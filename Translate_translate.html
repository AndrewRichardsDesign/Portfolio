Google Translate Redesign

Personal Project

A streamlined mobile translate experience, focused on Google's Messages app, with a more intuitive flow and more powerful features than currently seen in Gboard.

The problem

When people speaking different languages communicate with each other over text, translation is involved. Google integrated Translate into the Gboard (Google's mobile keyboard), but the current process for translating messages is slow, tedious, and does not provide permanent translations for users to refer back to throughout the conversation.

My goal

This project aims to shift translation from a burdensome task that hinders text conversation to an enabler of seemless, natural text conversations between different native speakers.

Why this project?

Drawing from much personal experience, it was extremely difficult to have natural conversations and maintain friendships with non-English speakers via text. I regularly had to re-translate forgotten messages to remember the topic we were discussing. I found that without messages that persist their translations, my friends and I could never get a complete understanding of the conversation as it progressed. We could either understand half the messages in our thread, or someone had to do the difficult job of committing to a language they didn't know, translating incoming and outgoing messages and only being able to read one message at a time in their native language.

As you can imagine, this situation caused friendships to fail.

Current Google translate flow in Gboard

The current process is long, slow, and doesn't preserve translations in the chat. Users have to advance through the Gboard Translate flow for each incoming and outgoing text. This process makes deep conversations particularly frustrating as the only chance users have to read is at the moment of translation and it is usually helpful to re-read a text as one writes a reply.

Translate individual messages

Starting by integrating into the Google Messages app, this concept allows users to select messages and bypass the Gboard flow, shortcutting to the Translate language modal.

Unlike the current Gboard functionality, this concept preserves translations, enabling a natural conversation for both parties. Now users can refer back to previous messages without re-translating to regain context and understanding.

Auto Translate

Rather than individually selecting or multi-selecting messages to translate, this concept automatically translates new incoming and outgoing messages. Besides the initial setup and potential translation inaccuracies, this is closest to a natural conversation.

Both users are able to type and read in their native languages without needing to translate each message they send and receive. Like the concept above, translations are preserved, so users can refer to previous messages as needed.

In this scenario, Andrew is receiving messages from Diana. He's already individually translated the first two messages into English but knows they will be talking for a while, so he decides to set up Auto Translate, entering from the contextual menu.

At the end of this scenario, Andrew wants to make sure his message sent in Spanish, so he taps it to reveal the message's timestamp and detail. He's also curious what his message looks like in Spanish, so he does an individual message translation.

Group messages and Auto Translate

When participating in a group chat with multiple languages, Auto Translate can be utilized with a bit more initial setup. In this scenario, Andrew is talking to two Spanish speakers and two English speakers. He enters the Auto Translate page and assigns a language to each person for "received" and "sent" messages. Since the English speakers can read his original messages, he leaves their translation toggles off.
