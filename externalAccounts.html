External Account Errors, Sign in, and Loading

USAA - Member Home

These design solutions communicate to members with specificity, clear actions and expectations. It deliniates between actions the member needs to take and when next steps are in USAA's hands. Plugging into the messaging framework that my team also developed, we're able to append messages to individual accounts, so members know exactly where the problems lie and eliminate repetitive steps.

A Typical Page With No Issues

For all examples, look for the Wells Fargo Account in the Banking section.

A member with no errors or requirements associated with external accounts will not see any messages or actions.

1. "Delayed"

Members may experience this error as they initially aggregate an external account. A placeholder account block displays at the end of an account list, instead of an account or multiple accounts at a particular institution. The account(s) load onto the page when the error resolves.

2. "Degraded or Disconnected"

These are examples of errors that USAA handles without member action. While in the error state, the account displays data (name, balance) that might be outdated because the data call failed. The balance displays in gray paired with the last known successful data call. At the time of this design, USAA was not able to dismiss messages, but ideally dismissal would be an option.

3. "Locked or Impeded"

These are examples of errors the member is required to resolve by taking an action at their external institution. The action may be refreshing a page or following more detailed instructions upon arrival to the other website.

4. "Prevented, Imported, Impaired, or Failed"

Four distinct errors exist with the same resolution. The 'Re-enter' button here brings members to a 3rd party tool to enter their credentials again, re-establishing a connection to their external account.

5. "Disabled, Discontinued, or Closed"

These errors communicate the permanent discontinuation of updates from their external institution. The balance is replaced by a status and the member has the option to remove their account via the 'manage' button or dismiss the message, keep the account visible, and retain access to their transaction history.

6. "Multi-Factor Accounts"

For all 'MFA' accounts, members are required to log in each time they visit USAA. This can be done via the 'update' button, which takes the member to our 3rd party tool. After logging in, the account displays data from the latest update, which could be many hours ago depending on where the member is in the daily update cycle, an unfortunate constraint of the system.

7. Logging in During the Daily Update

If a member logs in during the daily update process (while new data is importing), the balance and timestamp from the last update will display until the current process finishes.

8. Implications of an Unfortunate System Limitation

Upon login, if data loads successfully, the balance and timestamp will reflect the last daily update. This could have been 5 hours, for example, before the current log in session. A timestamp of '5 hours ago' would actually be accurate and not trigger an error. This timestamp would continue to increase until the next daily update.

The issue with this system limitation is that the account balance at an external institution may have changed at 7:00am, but since is changed after the daily update, USAA is not receiving the most up to date information, which could confuse or mislead members. For this reason, our design team was advocating for data calls at every log in.
